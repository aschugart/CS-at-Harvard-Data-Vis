Understanding the CS Experience at Harvard

By Alex Abrahams, Tommy O'Shea, and Annie Schugart

Since 2008, the number of computer science concentrators at Harvard has quadrupled.

Even more students are earning a secondary in computer science or simply exploring the field through a computer science class. It's becoming increasingly apparent: the computer science department is rapidly growing. But along with this academic growth comes a growing concern about the gender and racial gap within computer science.

With a sample size of over 900, we're exploring the opinions about the computer science experience at Harvard.

We're basing our analysis on data from the Women in Computer Science's survey, which obtained responses from a wide range of Harvard students (both computer science concentrators and non-concentrators, men and women, and varying races), as well as data obtained from Harvard's School of Engineering and Applied Sciences (home of the computer science department).
